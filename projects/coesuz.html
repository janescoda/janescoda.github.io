01 · Project

Turning an academic case into a believable product launch

Coesuz is an academic project developed as part of a master’s degree at ESAME Pharmaceutical Business School. The goal was to simulate the launch of an innovative product in the pharmaceutical and biotechnology sector. My contribution focused on creating the key visual elements: the 3D design of the product box, its animation, and the editing of the promotional video.

Based on the strategic requirements defined by the team, I conceptualised and modelled the packaging in 3D, looking for a balance between clarity, a contemporary look and alignment with the visual codes of the sector. From there, I developed 3D animations that show the box, its graphic details and its features in motion, turning it into a compelling piece for presentations and communication.

Finally, I took care of the promotional video editing, combining 3D animations with additional graphic and text layers to build a clear and professional narrative. The result is an audiovisual piece designed to reinforce the product’s positioning in an academic context and to simulate how it could be presented to potential clients, investors or internal committees.

The aim was not just to show a 3D box, but to build a full visual story that would feel at home in a real pharmaceutical launch presentation.
02 · 3D packaging

Packaging that speaks the language of healthcare

For the box design I worked with a clean, clinical aesthetic and a blue‑green palette to convey trust, technology and safety. The layout organises key information (name, dosage, indications and regulatory elements) in a way that is easy to scan both in static visuals and in motion.

I treated the packaging as if it were going to print: dieline, sides, barcodes and legibility at different sizes were all considered before moving to rendering and animation.

03 · Animation

Animating the box for presentations and video

The animation focused on showing the box from different angles, highlighting key panels and ensuring that text and visual codes remained legible throughout. The movement is smooth and controlled so that it can be paused at any moment during a pitch or presentation without losing clarity.
