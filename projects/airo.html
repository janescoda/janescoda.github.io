Airo – Universal public transport card

A university concept for a universal public transport card and digital service that rewards sustainable mobility and makes buses, trains, trams and metro feel like one connected system.

Client University project
Year 2023
Sector Mobility
My role Visual design & content
01 · Concept

A card that rewards everyday sustainable mobility

Airo was a university entrepreneurship and innovation proposal created with a group of classmates. The project envisioned a universal transport card for train, tram, bus and metro, designed to encourage the use of public transport through a reward system and promote a more sustainable and active lifestyle.

The main objective was to reduce private car use and CO₂ emissions in large cities while keeping public transport affordable, comfortable, safe and efficient.

To manage the experience, we proposed both a website and a mobile app where users could check routes, see the benefits they were unlocking and redeem rewards generated by their daily trips.

The concept also included a fully digital card, reducing the need for physical materials and aligning the service with environmental sustainability criteria. Bringing all transport modes together on a single medium simplified the user experience and reinforced the idea of a more connected, intelligent and environmentally friendly urban mobility system.

What Airo aims to solve
  • Fragmented tickets and apps across different public transport operators.
  • Lack of clear incentives for people to switch from private car to public transport.
  • Low visibility of the positive environmental impact of everyday mobility choices.
02 · Product

Mobile app to track trips, rewards and impact

The app is the main touchpoint where people see their journeys, earned points and available rewards. It brings together route planning, a loyalty system and a digital card so that the service lives entirely on the phone.

03 · Brand

A visual identity that supports a calm, trustworthy service

The visual identity was designed to feel clean, approachable and trustworthy — closer to a daily utility than a purely promotional campaign. The brand system had to work on the card, the app and future communication touchpoints.
