01 · Web & SEO

A Catalan website that is more than a translation

My work starts with the Catalan version of ikea.es: reviewing and updating key pages (home, categories, product pages, services and checkout) so they are not just a mirror of the Spanish site, but a clear and coherent experience for people buying from Catalonia.

Beyond language, I adjust structure, messages and navigation patterns so that users can find what they need quickly whether they arrive from the homepage, from search engines or from specific campaigns. The goal is that the Catalan site feels complete and fully maintained, not like a secondary version.

The objective is not only to translate content, but to offer a Catalan experience that is as easy to find and to use as the main site, with the same level of care.
SEO & UX focus
  • Balance between real search terms and IKEA’s brand voice in Catalan.
  • Clearer navigation paths between key categories and services.
  • Helpful messages around delivery, returns, availability and other “critical moments”.

What I do on the web side

  • Editorial review of key Catalan pages to spot inconsistencies, gaps and outdated content.
  • Rewriting copy to improve clarity and make the Catalan and Spanish versions feel equally solid.
  • On‑page SEO: meta titles, descriptions and slugs that match how people actually search in Catalonia.
  • Design of “internal menus” through filters and cross‑links between related categories.

Example of a category page in Catalan with adapted structure, copy and filters to match local search behaviour.

02 · Digital strategy

Guidelines, campaigns and email that speak consistently

To avoid reinventing the wheel with every campaign, I helped create a digital communication guide for Catalonia: formats for banners, promotional modules, service messages and layouts for web, app and mobile.

On top of this system, I develop local campaigns (promotions, services, parking, sustainability…) adapted to the available formats and coordinated with the global calendar so that the Catalan market feels aligned but relevant.

I also work on email marketing blocks specifically for Catalonia inside national newsletters, so people receive messages that match their location without losing brand consistency.

Key elements
  • Format guide for digital banners, modules and service messages.
  • Local campaigns aligned with IKEA’s global ecosystem.
  • Email blocks customised for the Catalan market inside national sends.

Digital communication guide that summarises available formats in the IKEA ecosystem and the user flows for presenting campaigns and activities.

03 · Stores

Local pages that reflect what happens in each store

Every store in Catalonia has its own local page with opening hours, services, pick‑up options, active promotions and practical information for the visit. I keep these pages updated so they match the reality of Badalona, Sabadell, Gran Via, Hospitalet and the urban stores.

SMÅLAND and IKEA Family activities are also published and reviewed every month, so families can easily see what is happening at their nearest store and plan their visit.

Impact in store
  • Clear local pages that connect web, workshops and services.
  • Promotions and services highlighted according to each location.
  • Better visibility for workshops and activities to strengthen the local community link.

Example of a local store page with location, services, links to workshops and practical information for the visit.

04 · Expansion

Supporting openings and refurbishments from digital

From the digital side I support expansions in Catalonia, from new stores to refurbishments and new formats. This means preparing web information, updating local pages, creating specific banners and making sure people understand what is new in their area from day one.

Recent work includes the opening of IKEA Granollers, the communication around the refurbishment of Badalona, and other planned changes in the region.

What we ensure
  • New stores appear correctly on the website from launch.
  • Refurbishments are communicated clearly in digital channels.
  • People know which services and access options are available at each location.

Web banners and modules used to communicate openings and refurbishments so people understand what changes in their store.

Results

What has improved
  • More people reach the Catalan version directly through search and campaigns.
  • Local campaigns are easier and faster to launch thanks to a clear format system.
  • Store pages and expansions are better represented online, matching what happens on site.
Takeaways

What I take away from this project

  • Localisation goes beyond language: adjusting category names, filters and messages changes how the brand is perceived in each market.
  • A good system saves future work: having clear templates and formats makes every new campaign faster and less chaotic.
  • Stores need realistic digital visibility: what we promise online has to match what people find in the store.
  • Measuring helps prioritise: looking at which modules and local campaigns perform best guides where to invest more effort.
